Eiffel Tower, Paris

Overview of Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, an architectural masterpiece in the heart of Paris, stands tall as a symbol of romance, innovation, and cultural identity. Completed in 1889 for the World's Fair, Gustave Eiffel's creation is a lattice ironwork marvel that draws millions of visitors yearly. Situated on the Champ de Mars, the tower offers breathtaking panoramic views of Paris from its observation decks. Beyond its structural grandeur, the Eiffel Tower serves as a multifunctional landmark, hosting cultural events and charmingly lighting up the Parisian skyline. Its purpose transcends its original World's Fair role, becoming an emblem of love and a must-visit destination for those seeking to experience the allure and sophistication of the City of Light.

History

Constructed between 1887 and 1889 for the 1889 World's Fair, the Eiffel Tower in Paris is a monumental feat of engineering designed by Gustave Eiffel in collaboration with Maurice Koechlin, Ã‰mile Nouguier, and architect Stephen Sauvestre. Unveiled on March 31, 1889, the tower's intricate lattice ironwork, composed of over 18,000 parts and 2.5 million rivets, represented a groundbreaking achievement in 19th-century engineering. Despite initial skepticism, the Eiffel Tower quickly became an iconic symbol of France, adorned with various decorations over the years. Designated as a historic monument, it continues to captivate millions with its panoramic views and enduring charm, embodying both architectural brilliance and the romantic spirit of Paris.

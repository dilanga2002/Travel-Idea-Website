Taj Mahal, Agra

Overview of Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, situated in Agra, India, stands as one of the most iconic symbols of love and architectural marvels in the world. Commissioned in the 17th century by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts millions of visitors annually. This magnificent white marble mausoleum is renowned for its exquisite craftsmanship, symmetry, and the ethereal beauty that changes with the daylight.

History

The Taj Mahal, a testament to eternal love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, was commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Over 22 years, a team of skilled artisans, led by chief architect Ustad Ahmad Lahori, meticulously crafted this marvel on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in Agra, India. The Taj Mahal's symmetrical layout, pristine white marble adorned with intricate inlay work, and its iconic dome flanked by minarets reflect a harmonious blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian influences. Beyond its architectural brilliance, the Taj Mahal stands as a poignant symbol of love, with Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal resting in eternal peace within its chambers.

Nuwara Eliya

Overview

Discover Sri Lanka's diverse beauty from Sigiriya's ancient marvel to Anuradhapura's sacred charm. Nuwara Eliya's misty hills and Galle Fort's history beckon. Yala National Park teems with wildlife, Mirissa's beaches allure, and Kandy adds cultural richness to this island paradise.

History

British Colonial Influence

Nuwara Eliya, nestled in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, bears the imprint of British colonial history. Established in the 19th century, this town served as a retreat for British colonizers seeking respite from the tropical lowlands. The architecture, including colonial-era mansions and a golf course, reflects this influence. Surrounded by lush tea plantations, Nuwara Eliya's cool climate and scenic beauty make it a charming testament to the bygone era of British Ceylon.
